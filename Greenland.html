Innovate & Code Workshop

Greenland High School

Tokha, Kathmandu

From Logic to Launch — A Complete CS Journey

The Innovate & Code (IC) Workshop was successfully organized at Greenland High School, Tokha, Kathmandu, as a three-day immersive learning program designed to introduce students to the foundations of computer science and artificial intelligence. This edition of the workshop focused specifically on Grades 5–7, bridging curiosity with core digital skills in a practical, age-appropriate manner.

The program was designed to move beyond traditional classroom learning by helping students understand how technology works behind the scenes. A key emphasis of the workshop was Artificial Intelligence (AI)—what it is, how it works, and how students can responsibly and effectively use AI in their daily life and academic work. Rather than treating AI as a mysterious tool, students explored its logic through simple explanations, real-world examples, and interactive discussions.

Over the three days, students learned about the basic principles of AI, including data, patterns, decision-making, and automation. They explored how AI tools can assist with tasks such as learning, research, creativity, and productivity—while also understanding important concepts like ethics, accuracy, and responsible use. Hands-on activities encouraged students to think critically, ask questions, and experiment with AI-powered tools in a guided environment.

The workshop fostered computational thinking, creativity, and confidence. Students shifted from being passive users of technology to active thinkers and problem solvers, gaining clarity on how AI and computer science can support their future learning journeys.

This program reflects our vision of making modern computer science education accessible, practical, and inspiring from an early age. The photos from the workshop capture students actively engaging, collaborating, and discovering the power of technology—moments that represent the beginning of their journey from logic to innovation.
